Bezirkshauptmann

District Administration in Upper Austria

Upper Austria is divided into 15 rural districts (Bezirke), each administered by a Bezirkshauptmannschaft. The person in charge is the Bezirkshauptmann or Bezirkshauptfrau, a senior career civil servant appointed by the state government.

The role exists because Austria devolves a wide range of state responsibilities to the district level. The Bezirkshauptmannschaft is the principal authority citizens deal with for most official matters: building permits, business licences, driving licences, vehicle registration, health and social services, environmental approvals, and more.

Because they are appointed rather than elected, district heads carry no party affiliation. They serve the state government regardless of which parties hold power in Linz or Vienna.

The three larger cities in Upper Austria (Linz, Steyr, and Wels) are Statutarstädte: they function as their own district, so no Bezirkshauptmann exists there. Instead, the elected Bürgermeister takes on the responsibilities that a BH would handle elsewhere.
